Differential Equations Research Project:
Epidemic Modelling

By Gage Broberg / January 31, 2022


My professor, Dr. Rundell, for differential equations at Texas A&M was fantastic and "diff E Q" was one of my favorite classes I have taken in the Math Department. He mentioned that we would be doing end of semester projects instead of an exam for our final, and when he mentioned the applications of differential equations to epidemic modelling in passing, I knew that's where I wanted to spend my time and effort. It seemed fitting having spent nearly all of my time at A&M in a pandemic!


The paper I produced is an analysis of some of the most basic to some of the more complex models used to predict and reflect on epidemics, with some of the final models being my own creations using the models I had researched as, well, a model...
